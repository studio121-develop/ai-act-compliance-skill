# Spain :es: — AI Act National Context

Spain holds a genuinely notable distinction in the AI Act story: it was the first EU country to launch a regulatory sandbox for artificial intelligence, in November 2022 — before the AI Act was even finalised. While other Member States were still debating the regulation's text in Council working groups, Spain was already testing what compliance might look like in practice. This head start gives Spain a unique perspective and a body of practical experience that other countries are now scrambling to replicate.

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-28

## Competent Authorities

### AEPD — Agencia Española de Protección de Datos
- **Role:** Spanish data protection authority — and much more in the AI context
- **AI Act relevance:** The AEPD has been the driving force behind Spain's AI regulatory sandbox and is the most active Spanish authority on AI governance. It enforces GDPR/LOPDGDD, provides AI-specific guidance, and has positioned itself as the natural lead authority for AI Act enforcement in Spain. The AEPD's proactive approach — publishing practical tools, running the sandbox, engaging with industry — sets it apart from many peer DPAs in Europe.
- **URL:** https://www.aepd.es

### INCIBE — Instituto Nacional de Ciberseguridad
- **Role:** National Cybersecurity Institute
- **AI Act relevance:** Responsible for cybersecurity aspects of AI systems, particularly the robustness and resilience requirements under the AI Act. INCIBE provides guidance and incident response support and is expected to contribute to conformity assessments for high-risk AI systems involving security components. Based in León, not Madrid — a deliberate decentralisation choice.
- **URL:** https://www.incibe.es

### SEDIA — Secretaría de Estado de Digitalización e Inteligencia Artificial
- **Role:** Secretary of State for Digitalisation and Artificial Intelligence
- **AI Act relevance:** The government body responsible for AI policy coordination, including the national AI strategy (ENIA) and digital transformation programs. SEDIA has been instrumental in positioning Spain as an AI governance leader and coordinates between regulatory authorities and the innovation ecosystem.
- **URL:** https://portal.mineco.gob.es/es-es/digitalizacionIA

### CNMC — Comisión Nacional de los Mercados y la Competencia
- **Role:** Competition and markets authority
- **AI Act relevance:** Relevant for AI systems in regulated markets and for competition implications of AI deployment. The CNMC has shown interest in algorithmic pricing and AI-driven market dynamics.
- **URL:** https://www.cnmc.es

## The Spanish AI Regulatory Sandbox — A Closer Look

Spain launched its AI sandbox (Entorno Controlado de Pruebas — "controlled testing environment") in November 2022, making it the first operational AI regulatory sandbox in the EU. This was not an accident — it was a deliberate strategy to influence the AI Act's sandbox provisions by generating practical experience.

### How It Works
- **Legal basis:** Royal Decree 817/2023 established the formal framework
- **Administered by:** The AEPD, with support from SEDIA
- **Scope:** Open to companies, research institutions, and public-sector bodies developing or deploying AI systems. Projects are selected through periodic calls
- **Duration:** Participants typically receive 12-18 months of supervised testing
- **What participants get:**
  - Dedicated regulatory guidance from AEPD experts
  - A controlled environment to test AI systems against emerging regulatory requirements
  - Feedback on compliance approaches before they become legally binding
  - Published results that benefit the wider ecosystem

### Results and Lessons
The sandbox has produced practical insights that informed both Spain's national approach and EU-level discussions:
- Real-world testing of risk classification methodologies
- Practical challenges in implementing transparency requirements
- Sector-specific compliance patterns (healthcare, finance, public administration)
- Evidence on proportionate compliance for SMEs versus large enterprises

### Why It Matters for Everyone
Spain's sandbox results are publicly shared and have influenced the AI Act's own sandbox provisions (Art. 57-62). If you are setting up an AI compliance program anywhere in the EU, the Spanish sandbox publications are worth reading — they contain practical knowledge that no amount of legal commentary can substitute.

## National AI Strategy — ENIA

The Estrategia Nacional de Inteligencia Artificial (ENIA), adopted in 2020, outlines Spain's AI ambitions:

- **Investment:** Significant public funding through the Recovery, Transformation and Resilience Plan (funded partly by NextGenerationEU)
- **Focus areas:** Spanish language AI (a strategic priority given 500+ million Spanish speakers globally), public administration, healthcare, agritech
- **Ethics and rights:** Strong emphasis on trustworthy AI, aligned with the EU approach
- **Talent:** Programs to expand AI education and attract international researchers
- **Digital Spain 2026:** Broader digital transformation agenda that frames AI strategy within national modernisation goals

## National Implementation Law

- **Status:** Under preparation (as of early 2026)
- **Key considerations:**
  - Spain's sandbox experience gives it a practical foundation that most Member States lack
  - The AEPD is expected to play a central role in the enforcement architecture
  - National provisions will likely build on the sandbox framework to create permanent compliance support structures
  - Spain has indicated interest in a relatively streamlined approach, leveraging existing institutional capacity rather than creating new bodies

## Related National Regulations

### LOPDGDD — Ley Orgánica de Protección de Datos y Garantía de los Derechos Digitales (2018)
Spain's GDPR implementation law, with a notable addition: it includes a section on digital rights guarantees (Título X) that go beyond standard data protection. Relevant provisions include:
- Right to digital education (Art. 83)
- Right to digital security (Art. 82)
- Rights in the employment context (Arts. 87-91) — relevant for workplace AI
- Right to not be subject to automated individual decisions (Art. 22, mirroring GDPR Art. 22)

### Carta de Derechos Digitales (2021)
Spain's Digital Rights Charter — a non-binding but politically significant document that articulates rights in the digital age. Particularly relevant sections:
- **Right to algorithmic transparency:** Individuals have the right to know when decisions affecting them involve algorithmic processing
- **Right to non-discrimination in the digital environment:** AI systems must not produce discriminatory outcomes
- **Rights in relation to AI:** A dedicated section addressing AI-specific rights, including the right to human oversight of significant AI decisions
- While not legally binding, the Charter signals the direction of Spanish policy and influences regulatory interpretation. Courts and regulators reference it increasingly.

### Consumer Protection (Ley General para la Defensa de los Consumidores y Usuarios)
Consumer protection obligations apply alongside AI Act transparency requirements. AI-generated content in commercial contexts must comply with both frameworks.

## SME Landscape and Support

Spain's economy relies heavily on SMEs (over 99% of businesses), making accessible compliance support critical:

- **AEPD tools:** The AEPD publishes free compliance tools, guides, and checklists specifically designed for smaller organisations. Its "Facilita" tool for GDPR has been praised for usability — expect similar tools for AI Act compliance.
- **Red.es:** Public entity promoting digital economy, offers funding and support for AI adoption by SMEs
- **CDTI:** Centro para el Desarrollo Tecnológico e Industrial provides grants and loans for technology development, including AI projects
- **Aceleradora ENIA:** AI-specific acceleration programs under the national strategy
- **Regional programs:** Spain's autonomous communities (Comunidades Autónomas) have their own digital innovation programs, particularly Catalonia, Madrid, Basque Country, and Andalusia
